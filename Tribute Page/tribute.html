Obafemi Awolowo

A President Nigeria never had

Here's a time line of Obafemi's Awolowo's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Ikenne, ogun State
  • 1933 - Struggled to make ends meeet. He did menial jobs to pay for school. "
  • 1937 - Marries wife of 50 years Hannah (popularly known as H.I.D).
  • 1944 - Enrols in the University of London to study Law. Also founded the Egbe Omo Oduduwa (Yoruba: "Society of the Descendants of Oduduwa")
  • 1947 - He wrote the influential "Path to Nigerian Freedom in which he made his case for the need of a Federal form of government in an independent Nigeria.
  • 1947 - He returned to Ibadan, Nigeria to practise Law.
  • 1948 - Founded Egbe Omo Oduduwa in Nigeria.
  • 1950 - He founded a political party, the Action Group with some of the Egbe's members as its nucleus.
  • 1951 - The party won the first election held in the Western Region, one of the colony's three administrative divisions and Awolowo later served as Leader of government business and minister for Local government structure.
  • 1954 - Between 1954-1959, as Premier of the Western Region, Awolowo worked to improve education, social services and agriculture practices, implementing many progressive policies. Notably, his administration introduced programs that provided free healthcare for children and free universal primary education.
  • 1959 - The first television in Africa was established in the Western Region. He later resigned the Premiership to stand for election which his party lost and found himself Leader of the Opposition in the Federal House of Represntatives.
  • 1960 - Nigeria achieves independence
  • 1963 - There was unrest on the political scene. He was tried and convicted of conspiracy to overthrow the government and was sentenced to ten years."
  • 1966 - He was released after a military coup took place.
  • 1967 - Between 1967-1970, he was Federal commissioner for Finance and Vice chairman of the Federal Executive Council.
  • 1971 - He resigned to protest the Government's scontinuation of military rule. He became Chancellor of University of Ife (now Obafemi Awolowo University) and Ahmadu Bello University.
  • 1978 - Unity Party of Nigeria was founded.
  • 1979 - In 1979 & 1983, he ran for President as the candidate of the Unity Party of Nigeria losing to Shehu Shagari.
  • 1983 - He returned to private life.
  • 1987 - He died in Ikenne.
